Halfway through a season I sat on the tailgate and looked at the back
of the truck and realized I had built, by accident, a kind of small
museum of things that make a long field day survivable. None of them
are rocks.

## The list, mostly honest

- a dented thermos that lost its lid in 2024 and has refused to leak
  ever since
- two bandanas, one of which I will eventually sacrifice to a thin
  section bag
- a paperback I never read, kept exclusively to lend
- one cheap pair of reading glasses, because the field notebook
  conspires against tired eyes
- a small jar of instant coffee that stays in the truck even when I
  hate it
- a ziplock bag of receipts, ostensibly for accounting, mostly archival

## Why it matters

The truck inventory is the part of fieldwork no one writes about. It
is also the part that decides whether the day ends at three or seven.
A spare pair of socks is not a research output, but its absence is.

I think about this when I design tools. The best tools are the ones
that make the small obvious things impossible to forget. Spare socks
for the workflow.

## Untracked

I keep a gas-station notebook in the glovebox that nobody ever sees,
where I write down the names of cafés that remembered my order, and
the names of dogs I met at gas stations. It is the most honest
notebook I keep, and it is the one I'd save first if the truck caught
fire.
